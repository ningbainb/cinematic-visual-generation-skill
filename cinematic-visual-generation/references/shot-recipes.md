# Shot Recipes

Use one recipe as a starting point, then tailor it to the story. Do not combine recipes indiscriminately.

## Grounded independent drama

Use this as the default when a user says the result feels “too AI” or too polished.

- Frame: eye-level 35–50 mm frame, a mildly awkward crop or partial occlusion is welcome; use negative space only when the location naturally creates it.
- Light: available light from a window, overhead fluorescent, streetlamp, or train carriage. Keep shadows imperfect and exposure slightly uneven where the source demands it.
- Performance: catch a small physical action instead of a pose—adjusting a scarf, wiping rain from a hand, looking away, waiting mid-step. Keep emotion contained.
- Look: neutral low-saturation color, normal skin texture, moderate depth of field, minor focus or motion imperfection. Do not add fog, bloom, strong grain, high-contrast rim light, or color contrast unless the scene contains a source for it.
- Prompt suffix: `observational independent-film still, available light, ordinary lived-in location, unretouched skin, restrained color, no editorial posing, no glossy finish, no poster composition`.

## Rainy platform baseline

Use this reference for the successful restrained night-station look. Keep the principles when changing the location or story.

- Subject: one ordinary person, viewed from the side or behind, caught waiting or looking rather than performing emotion.
- Place: a modest, slightly worn transit space—chipped paint, aged bench, damp tile, uneven puddles, distant residential windows. Let the setting carry the mood.
- Frame: medium-wide, eye-level, 40 mm; allow a pillar, bench, or roof edge to interrupt the frame. Keep the person off-centre and looking away from the camera.
- Light: one overhead fluorescent plus dim distance lamps; retain mixed colour temperature and uneven exposure. Do not introduce neon, haze, headlight flares, or strong rim light.
- Surface detail: wet hair against the forehead, creased coat, canvas tote, stained floor, fine rain visible only inside the fluorescent pool.
- Avoid: stylised grief, beautified rain, fashion styling, immaculate locations, symmetry, saturated cyan, amber contrast, heavy grain, or hyper-clean detail.

```text
[Person, age range, ordinary clothing] waits at [worn everyday transit location] in light rain at night. They are [small, interrupted action] and look away from camera.
Eye-level medium-wide 40 mm frame, partly occluded by [real foreground object], naturally off-centre. Available [fluorescent/street/train] light only, mixed colour temperature, soft uneven exposure, neutral low-saturation blue-grey and muted practical colours. Unretouched skin, damp hair, creased fabric, stained tiles and small puddles.
Observational independent-film still, believable lived-in location. No editorial pose, no glossy finish, no artificial fog, no stylised colour grade, no dramatic light rays, no poster composition, no text or watermark.
```

## Quiet interior drama

- Frame: medium close-up through a doorway or foreground object; subject off-center with meaningful negative space.
- Light: one large side window or practical lamp, soft falloff, restrained highlights.
- Look: muted warm neutrals, subtle grain, shallow but believable depth of field.
- Best for: grief, anticipation, private decisions, domestic tension.

## Night-city neo-noir

- Frame: medium-wide or close-up with wet pavement, glass reflections, or partial occlusion.
- Light: motivated neon and streetlight, hard edge light, selective face visibility.
- Look: deep cyan, magenta, amber, or sodium-vapor accents; smoke or rain only if the scene earns it.
- Best for: pursuit, alienation, urban mystery, after-hours energy.

## Epic landscape or science fiction

- Frame: wide establishing shot; a small readable subject anchors the foreground or lower third.
- Light: low sun, eclipse, storm break, or artificial source with clear scale cues.
- Look: controlled atmospheric perspective, a limited palette, layered depth.
- Best for: awe, danger, discovery, solitude.

## Kinetic action

- Frame: low angle, near subject, diagonal movement across the composition.
- Camera: slight motion blur on the environment while the narrative focal point remains legible.
- Light: backlight or hard side light to separate silhouettes.
- Best for: escape, sport, impact, chase. Avoid blur everywhere.

## Premium product hero

- Frame: three-quarter product view with room for the silhouette; one tactile material detail in focus.
- Light: large soft key plus a narrow rim or reflected highlight that describes form.
- Look: intentional set design, restrained palette, clean but not sterile finish.
- Best for: fragrance, watches, cars, technology, food.

## Corrective moves

| Symptom | Make this change |
| --- | --- |
| Looks like a generic poster | Add a specific action, story clue, and motivated light source; remove broad quality adjectives. |
| Subject is lost | Specify shot size, subject placement, contrast priority, and one focal detail. |
| Feels overprocessed | Remove redundant effects, reduce saturation, and request natural texture with controlled highlights. |
| Feels flat | Add foreground/midground/background layers and directional light or shadow. |
| Mood contradicts scene | Replace palette and lighting before changing the subject or adding more props. |
